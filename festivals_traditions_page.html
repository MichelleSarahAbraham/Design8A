We will be talking about the festivals of Valencia. Here I will be talking about the two most popular festivals in Valencia.

The first is Las Fallas. Las Fallas is an extremely well-known festival that takes place from 15-19 March. It celebrates the coming of spring and the beauty of Valencian art and culture and its long Silk Roads history. Las Fallas is celebrated by many marching bands going around the city and parading along the roads of Valencia. Street food is also served along the roads. They celebrate the fact that Valencia is still one of the only places where silk is made through traditional looms from the 18th century. Not only that, but local artists in Valencia collaborate and create a giant sculpture over a period of months prior to the festival symbolizing any major societal issue and at the end of the festival is placed in the town square to be burnt. The burning of these sculptures represents the rebirth of social activity.

The second festival which is also quite popular is La Tomatina. It is celebrated on the last Wednesday of August and actually started from a street fight back in 1945 where people swung tomatoes at each other. Here people gather in Buñol, a town in Valencia and start throwing tomatoes at each other. They first crush the tomatoes and then throw it as to not harm anyone.
